Visualization Design (What-Why-How)

In the first two lectures, you are introduced to the what-why-how framework by Tamara Munzner:

  • what? (what kind of data is it?)
  • why? (which tasks do you want to perform on the data?)
  • how? (how to encode data with marks & channels?)

In the assignment, you will perform one full design cycle: You will formally describe a dataset in terms of data types. You will develop a research question towards the data and break it down into analysis tasks. And finally you will choose suitable encodings to express the data, such that the derived analysis tasks are supported and the research question answered. The dataset for this assignment is about 120 years of olypmic history: athletes and results (https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/heesoo37/120-years-of-olympic-history-athletes-and-results).

Tasks

Task 1: The What (25%)

Consider the contents of the athlete_events.csv. Determine the dataset type, and, for all attributes, the attribute types (categorical, ordinal, quantitative). Include the ranges of quantitative types, the possible attributes in order for ordinal attributes, and at least three different examples for categorical attributes.

Write your answer in the file "what.md".

Task 2: The Why (25%)

Part 1

Data scientists are interested in answering the following two questions:

  • Which teams are best at athletics and did this change over time?
  • Does the nationality and the location of the games have an influence on a players performance?

Analyze the type of task in the why of the what-why-how framework (Lecture 1: Slides 42 - 47), by determining at least one target - action pair for each question.

Write your answers in the file "why.md".

Part 2

Now think of two questions that you could answer with this dataset.

Write them in the file "why.md".

Task 3: The How (50%)

Consider only the first part of the scientist's first question in Task 2.1: Which teams are best at athletics? Please, implement either a scatter plot or bar chart to answer the question. A good measure to access how well each team does could be the number of medals the team won. Write your implementation into the index.js. The data has already been loaded for you. You can have a look at the structure of the data in the file athlete_events.csv or the developer console of your browser.

Write your reasoning as to why you chose the one visualization over the other and the marks and channels in your implementation in the file "how.md".
